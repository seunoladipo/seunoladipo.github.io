About me

I am an environmental scientist with a strong background in forestry, spatial science, and GIS-based environmental analysis. My academic and professional work focuses on understanding how forest and landscape management strategies can enhance biodiversity, ecosystem services, and resilience under climate change.

"Forests are among our most complex natural systems — and our most important allies against a changing climate."

My interdisciplinary training spans ecology, geospatial modelling, and environmental policy, allowing me to bridge the gap between scientific research and practical conservation strategies. I am actively seeking PhD opportunities and research collaborations that push the frontier of forest science.

About me

Scientist. Mapper.
Changemaker.

I am an environmental scientist and PhD candidate at the University of Virginia, where my research focuses on how urban green spaces can be optimally sited and managed to deliver maximum ecosystem services — from carbon sequestration and stormwater management to biodiversity support and urban cooling.

My academic journey has taken me across four continents. I completed a fully funded Erasmus Mundus MSc in Spatial Sciences (Islands and Sustainability) jointly at the University of Groningen in the Netherlands and the University of Las Palmas in Gran Canaria, Spain — where my thesis on CO₂ sink identification in Macaronesian island landscapes was published in a Q1 journal within three months of completion.

"Forests and green spaces are not merely scenery — they are critical infrastructure for a liveable planet."

Before my PhD, I worked as a Geospatial Researcher at the Institute of Oceanography and Global Change in Gran Canaria, a Research Scientist at the Forestry Research Institute of Nigeria, and a GIS Specialist intern at the Centre for Space Research and Application (CESRA) in Akure. Across all these roles, spatial science has been the thread connecting ecology, policy, and practice.

Beyond research, I care deeply about community — from co-founding literacy programmes during COVID-19 lockdowns to serving as a Google Local Guide (Level 8) who has added over 4,000 missing places and 200 roads across Africa, Asia, and Europe to improve how communities are seen on the map.

I am fluent in English, with intermediate proficiency in Spanish and Dutch — a reflection of the international nature of my academic and professional career. I am open to research collaborations, speaking engagements, and PhD co-supervision opportunities.

University of Virginia · PhD Research
Prioritization & Optimization Approaches for Ecosystem Service Provision in Urban Green Spaces
A systematic investigation into how cities can strategically site and design green spaces to maximise stacked ecosystem services — cooling, carbon, biodiversity, and wellbeing — using spatial decision-support models.
  • Conducted a systematic literature review assessing current green space siting practices across US cities
  • Analysed decision-support tools, spatial models, and data inputs guiding green space placement
  • Developing a framework to assess and compare ecosystem services across alternative siting scenarios

University of Las Palmas & Groningen · MSc Thesis
Methodology for Selecting Potential CO₂ Sink in Macaronesia Island: The Case of Gran Canaria
Developed a reproducible GIS-based methodology for identifying optimal afforestation and reforestation sites in Macaronesian island landscapes, with carbon sequestration and ecological suitability as primary criteria. Published in a Q1 journal within 3 months of thesis completion.
  • Conducted land suitability analysis to identify optimal locations for green space and carbon sinks
  • Analysed greenhouse gas emissions across all 8 Canary Islands, identifying three major emission clusters
  • Published peer-reviewed results in Trees, Forests and People (Q1 Journal)

University of Groningen & Learning Hub Friesland
Integrating Backcasting Methods in Island Schools: Sustainable Transport
An interdisciplinary educational research project embedding sustainability thinking — specifically backcasting methodology — into school curricula in island communities across Greece, Iceland, and the Netherlands.
  • Designed and facilitated focus group discussions with secondary school students in Astypalaia, Hrisey, and Vlieland
  • Analysed focus group transcripts using Atlas.ti to extract thematic patterns
  • Translated empirical insights into evidence-based curriculum recommendations

Federal University of Technology Akure · BSc Thesis
Assessment of Geospatial Distribution of Sawmills in Ife-East LGA, Osun State, Nigeria
A spatial investigation into informal sawmill operations in southwestern Nigeria, examining geographic clustering, proximity to forest reserves, and implications for deforestation management policy.
  • Designed survey instruments and conducted field surveys across Ife-East Local Government Area
  • Analysed and mapped sawmill spatial distribution using QGIS, ArcMap, and Google Earth Engine
  • Developed feasible policy recommendations subsequently published in a peer-reviewed journal
